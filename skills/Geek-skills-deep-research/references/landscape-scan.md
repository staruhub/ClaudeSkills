# Landscape Scan V8

A landscape scan is not always an academic literature scan.
It is a **mapping step** used when the domain is broad, unfamiliar, or structurally messy.

## Choose the right landscape source

### Academic / scientific topic
Use papers, reviews, datasets, trial registries.

### Technical ecosystem topic
Use official docs, standards, repos, design docs, benchmark pages, release notes.

### Market / vendor topic
Use filings, official product docs, pricing, customer evidence, credible research, analyst notes.

### Policy / regulation topic
Use laws, regulator guidance, court/agency materials, policy trackers, official frameworks.

## Goal
Produce a short map of:
- major clusters / camps / approaches
- leading entities or sources in each cluster
- where the evidence is strongest / weakest
- candidate expert roles or research threads

## When to skip
Skip the scan when the task is already narrow and well scoped.

## Output
`workspace/landscape-scan.md`
with:
- clusters
- key sources/entities per cluster
- suggested research threads
- uncertainty notes

The scan is a planning aid, not a citation shortcut.
Any source you later cite still needs normal verification.

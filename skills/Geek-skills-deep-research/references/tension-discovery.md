# Tension Discovery V8

Use this only when the topic is likely to hide a misleading default narrative.

## Good fits
- hype-heavy technologies
- procurement / strategy / policy choices
- topics with strong online consensus but weak operational evidence
- topics where benefits are over-advertised or costs are under-discussed

## Not a good fit
- neutral descriptive explainers
- routine overviews with little controversy
- narrow tasks where the user simply wants current facts

## Three useful probes

1. **Concept confusion**
   - Is one label hiding multiple mechanisms?
   - Example: “AI agent” could mean chat assistant, workflow bot, or autonomous operator.

2. **Assumption check**
   - What is everyone casually assuming is true?
   - What evidence would actually be needed to support that assumption?

3. **Effect vs mechanism**
   - People say it works. Do they know *why* it works?
   - Could the reported effect be real while the claimed cause is wrong?

## Output
Write a short `tension-scan.md` with 1-3 candidate tensions.
Each tension should be testable, not theatrical.

Bad: “Everything people believe about X is wrong.”
Good: “The main benefit of X may come more from Y than from the commonly cited Z.”
